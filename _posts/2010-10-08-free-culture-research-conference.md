The [Free Culture Research Conference](http://wikis.fu-berlin.de/display/fcrc/Home) is happening right now at the Freie Universität in Berlin. I’m here and [live-blogging it](http://blogs.fu-berlin.de/fcrc/) right today and tomorrow, and posting [tweets](https://search.twitter.com/search?q=%23fcrc) and [dents](https://identi.ca/tag/fcrc) tagged #fcrc, and I’ll probably post some kind of wrap-up later.

**UPDATE**: Whew, it’s over. I posted wrap-ups of [Geert Lovink’s keynote](http://blogs.fu-berlin.de/fcrc/2010/10/08/geert-lovink-keynote-after-the-critique-of-free-and-open-alternative-platforms-and-revenue-models/), [a panel on Wikimedia, government resources and the public domain](http://blogs.fu-berlin.de/fcrc/2010/10/08/wikimedia-panel-government-works-in-the-public-domain/), and [the COMMUNIA panel on the relationship between research and policy](http://blogs.fu-berlin.de/fcrc/2010/10/09/communia-panel-free-culture-research-and-policy/).
